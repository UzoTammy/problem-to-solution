Network Fundamentals

Hosts

Hosts is any device that can send or receive traffic (data in various forms) over a network.

A host can be either a server or a client depending on what role it is playing within the network.

A client is the host that makes a request from a server/sends traffic, while a server is the host that will respond.

These days alot of devices are hosts. Smart TV to Smart watch, Robots, Mobile phones etc

IP Address

This serves as the identity of the host. They are a 32 bits set of 0s and 1s grouped in four octets. It is assigned within the network.

Sample IP address in bits: 10010101110010010100101001001100

IP address grouped: 10010101 11001001 01001010 01001100 -> 149.201.74.76

It is structured by IETF (Internet Engineering Task Force) and regulated by IANA (Internet Assigned Number Authority) who also allocates to nations and regions. ISPs (Internet Service providers) are responsible for sales and distribution.

IP address can be likened to a person's assigned phone number and the Mac address as the person's name. Also, when traffic is sent over the network, the data pack will have source and destination IP Addresses. Similar to a person knowing where he is and where he is going.

IP address is hierachically assigned

What does this mean? An organization can obtain a particular range of IP address say for Acme Inc. they are assigned 10.X.X.X, the org will decide how to appropraite the IPs within their network. They have London, Tokyo and New York offices and decided to assign as follows 10.10.X.X, 10.20.X.X and 10.30.X.X respectively. Also in each office, they have R&D, Marketing and Engineering and further assigned as 10.10.40.X, 10.10.50.X, 10.10.60.X respectively and repeat same for Tokyo and New York
